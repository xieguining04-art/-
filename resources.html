Practical shipping guides for importers.

Prepare better cargo information, compare freight options and reduce delays before shipment.

QUOTE PREPARATION

What to include in a freight inquiry

Origin, destination, commodity, packing, dimensions, gross weight, quantity, Incoterm and target shipping date.

OCEAN FREIGHT

FCL versus LCL

FCL gives dedicated container space; LCL combines cargo from multiple shippers. The best choice depends on volume, urgency and handling needs.

AIR FREIGHT

Chargeable weight basics

Air freight charges compare actual weight with volumetric weight. Accurate carton dimensions are essential for a reliable quote.

INCOTERMS

EXW, FOB and DDP

These terms define who handles pickup, export, freight, import and delivery. Confirm the term before comparing quotations.

PACKING

Reduce damage and delays

Use suitable cartons, pallets or wooden cases, clear marks and accurate packing lists—especially for fragile, heavy or irregular cargo.

SUPPLIER CONSOLIDATION

Combining multiple orders

Consolidation can simplify export handling and reduce duplicated transport, but requires clear supplier schedules and cargo identification.
